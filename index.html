Steam Locomotive Class

The steam locomotive belongs to the 15F class. Approximately 255 locomotives of this class were built during production

The locomotive class can be identified by its wheel arrangment. This locomotive has a wheel arrangement of 4-8-2.

This arrangement means that there are 4 small wheels at the front of the locomotive, 8 large driving wheels in the middle and 2 small wheels at the rear of the locomotive.

Some History of the Locomotive class

This class of locomotives was built between 1938 and 1947 by four different engineering firms.

The four firms were Berliner Maschinenbau, Henschel & Son, North British Locomotive Company (NBL), and Beyer, Peacock & Company.

This specific locomotive, 15F fleet number: 3117, named Katryn, was built by the North British Locomotive Company (NBL), making her approximately 80 years old.

Some statistics for curiosity

The locomotive has a top speed of approximately 90 km/h, but for safety reasons there are speed limits in place on the railway line. The fastest we are permitted to go is approximately 45 km/h.

The locomotive has a maximum hauling capacity of 2 140 Tons

The locomotive's fire is lit approximately 8 to 12 hours before the locomotive enters service.

Around 1 Ton of coal is used to raise steam, while around 3 Tons of coal are consumed during a typical operating day consisting of two return trips to Pretoria Station.

Depending on whether the train is climbing a steep gradient or travelling on level track, it can consume anywhere between 170 to 280 liters of water per kilometer travelled.
